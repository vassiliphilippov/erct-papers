Abstract

In a randomized field trial with 2,850 seventh-grade mathematics students, the authors evaluated whether an educational technology intervention (ASSISTments) increased mathematics learning when used for homework. The intervention provided automated feedback and hints for students, while delivering organized performance data to teachers so they could adapt their instruction. Results showed significant gains in math test scores, especially for students with lower prior achievement.

ERCT Criteria Breakdown

  • Level 1 Criteria

    • C

      Class-level RCT

      • Randomization occurred at the school level, exceeding the class-level requirement.
      • Entire schools were assigned to treatment or control groups, avoiding contamination within classes.
      • "Schools were placed into pairs and randomly assigned to treatment and control." (p. 6)
    • E

      Exam-based Assessment

      • Used the TerraNova standardized test, a widely recognized assessment tool
      • Results were based on externally validated exams, not custom-made tests.
      • "Scores from the TerraNova Common Core assessment mathematics test were used as a primary outcome measure." (p. 5)
    • T

      Term Duration

      • The intervention lasted a full academic year.
      • The study spanned an entire seventh-grade cycle, exceeding the one-term requirement.
      • "Schools were placed into pairs and randomly assigned to treatment and control." (p. 6)
    • D

      Documented Control Group

      • Control group demographics and "business-as-usual" practices were clearly documented.
      • Baseline performance and group details were outlined in detail, ensuring transparency.
      • "Control group teachers continued with existing homework practices." (pp. 2–3, 4–5, Table 1)

  • Level 2 Criteria

    • S

      School-level RCT

      • Entire schools were randomized to treatment or control groups.
      • Entire schools were assigned to treatment or control groups, avoiding contamination within classes.
      • "Schools were placed into pairs and randomly assigned to treatment and control." (p. 6)
    • A

      Exam-based Assessment

      • The study measured only mathematics outcomes and did not assess other subjects like reading or science.
      • Evaluating only one subject limits the generalizability of the findings and does not capture potential unintended impacts on other academic areas.
      • "Scores from the TerraNova Common Core assessment mathematics test were used."
    • Y

      Year Duration

      • The study spanned a full academic year, ensuring long-term measurement.
      • Conducting the intervention over an entire school year allows researchers to observe sustained impacts and avoids the limitations of short-term studies.
      • "The intervention spanned most or all of the academic year." (p. 4)
    • B

      Balanced Resources

      • The treatment group received additional resources (e.g., professional development), which were not provided to the control group.
      • Unequal distribution of resources between treatment and control groups introduces bias, as the observed effects could stem from the additional support rather than the intervention itself.
      • “The control group continued with existing homework practices … teachers were not able to use ASSISTments…” (p. 2)

  • Level 3 Criteria

    • G

      Graduation Tracking

      • The study measured outcomes only at the end of seventh grade and did not follow students through to graduation.
      • Entire schools were assigned to treatment or control groups, avoiding contamination within classes.
      • "Schools were placed into pairs and randomly assigned to treatment and control." (p. 6)
    • R

      Reproduced Results

      • The study was not independently replicated by another research team.
      • Replication by an independent team ensures findings are robust and not specific to the context or original research team.
      • "Three prior research studies … showed that ASSISTments could have an effect on learning … (Mendicino ... Kelly ... Singh ...)." (p. 2)
    • I

      Independent Conduct

      • Conducted independently by researchers not affiliated with the ASSISTments developers.
      • Independence from the intervention’s creators eliminates conflicts of interest and enhances the reliability of the results.
      • "None of the authors has a financial interest in ASSISTments ... We especially thank Neil and Cristina Heffernan, the creators." (p. 10)
    • P

      Pre-Registered Protocol

      • The study was not pre-registered on any official platform.
      • Pre-registration increases transparency by committing researchers to specific hypotheses and methodologies before data collection, reducing the potential for bias.
      • No mention of pre-registration in the study.
